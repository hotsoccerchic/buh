Becky The Giant Raccoon

Published 7/14/2020




Becky was a rescued after sustaining an injury that made it difficult for her to walk. After spending some time with an elderly woman, her size grew to be tremendous due to too many pistachios and marshmallows. She quickly went viral after a video of an obese raccoon coming out of a little house made the rounds on Facebook. Fortunately, Becky the Raccoon is much healthier now and helps educate people about wildlife rescues.

Raccoons actually should not be kept like this unless the person keeping them is a wildlife rehabilitation expert. They carry many diseases and can wreak havoc on somebody's home as they are masterful bandits of the animal world. If you find an injured raccoon and want to help it, call your local wildlife rehabilitation institutions and they can handle the rest!

So how do you rehab an abandoned or injured raccoon? That depends on if it's a baby or an adult. If it's a baby, you might want to leave it alone in case its mother comes back for it. Try to keep it warm and hydrated, as baby raccoons are prone to succumbing to dehydration and hypothermia. Do NOT feed it human food- instead, call a wildlife rehabilitation center and ask what you can feed it.

Handling a raccoon is legal in most US states, but it's illegal to keep them for yourself. Your best option is to reach out to a licensed wildlife professional.

If you decide to handle an abandoned raccoon, make sure you're wearing gloves! This will protect you against biting and scratching, which is not only painful but also dangerous due to risk of diseases like rabies.
